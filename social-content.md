## Account 4 — Data Operations / Annotation Lead

### LinkedIn

People underestimate how much annotation quality matters for Physical AI.

Surface-level labels — "picks up cup", "opens door" — aren't enough to train a useful robotics policy.

What actually matters:

→ Hand-object contact points and grasp type at each frame
→ Intent and sub-goal segmentation across the full task sequence
→ Failure modes and recovery actions labeled separately
→ Scene graph relationships — what's near what, what's being used, what changed
→ Hierarchical structure — step, sub-task, task, session

Getting this right at scale requires both good tooling and deep domain understanding. It's not a commodity annotation job.

At MyTron Labs we've built our annotation pipeline around Physical AI requirements specifically — not repurposed from image classification or NLP workflows.

If you're building datasets for robotics or wearable AI and want to talk annotation methodology, my DMs are open.
